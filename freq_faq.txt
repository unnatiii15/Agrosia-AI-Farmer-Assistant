Question: How do I prevent poultry diseases naturally?
Answer: Keep poultry sheds clean and well-ventilated, use herbal feed additives like garlic and neem, vaccinate as recommended, provide clean water, and separate sick birds promptly.

Question: How can I improve egg production in chickens?
Answer: Provide a balanced diet with calcium-rich feed, maintain clean water, ensure proper lighting, keep sheds hygienic, and vaccinate regularly.

Question: How can I prevent locust swarms in my area?
Answer: Regularly monitor your fields, coordinate with local agriculture offices, use neem-based sprays or approved pesticides, and set up barrier nets. Early reporting of swarms helps control spread.

Question: What natural methods can I use to control whiteflies?
Answer: Introduce predatory insects like ladybugs or parasitic wasps, use yellow sticky traps, and spray neem or garlic-chili extracts. Avoid overuse of chemical pesticides.

Question: How do I prevent stem borer damage in paddy naturally?
Answer: Plant resistant varieties, keep field edges clean, release Trichogramma wasps, and remove affected stems early.

Question: How can I save water during irrigation?
Answer: Use drip irrigation or sprinklers, apply mulch to retain soil moisture, irrigate early morning or late evening, and schedule watering based on soil moisture measurements.

Question: How do I prevent waterlogging in lowland fields?
Answer: Build proper drainage channels, use raised beds, avoid over-irrigation, and plant water-tolerant crops.

Question: How do I apply for PMFBY insurance after crop damage?
Answer: Contact your insurance agent or local agriculture office, submit crop details and damage proof within the claim period, and follow instructions for inspection and compensation.

Question: How can women farmers access government schemes?
Answer: Schemes like MKSP provide training, subsidies, and financial support. Register at local KVKs or state agriculture offices and submit necessary ID and land documents.

Question: How do I prepare vermicompost at home?
Answer: Collect organic waste, shred it, layer it with bedding like coconut coir, introduce earthworms, maintain moisture, and harvest compost after 2–3 months.

Question: How can I control pests without chemicals?
Answer: Use neem oil sprays, introduce beneficial insects, use trap crops, and maintain field hygiene. Crop rotation also reduces pest buildup.

Question: How can I protect crops from frost?
Answer: Use mulching, row covers, plastic sheets, or greenhouse structures. Plant frost-tolerant varieties where possible.

Question: What should I do during a drought?
Answer: Mulch soil, irrigate efficiently using drip systems, plant drought-resistant crops, and store rainwater. Coordinate with local agriculture offices for relief schemes.
